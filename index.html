About

For me it was always about Design.

  • 2005-2007

    Drawing board, inks and pencils

    While on High School, I enrolled in an art academy in order to learn architectural and still life pencil drawing.

  • July 2013

    University years

    My BS degree in Informatics was focused on design, this time though with an emphasis on design and evaluation of user-centered software, human-computer interaction, interaction design.

  • January 2014

    Moving to UK

    Since moving to the UK, my design horizons have been opened up and my own personal challenge is to find ways to combine function and aesthetics in experiences that are shared online.

  • January 2014 - September 2015

    From Jr Front-End developer UX Engineer

    It was my choice to start my career as a frontend developer, driven by a belief that magic in digital products comes from a recipe of beautiful design and elegant code. Besides you need to learn the limitations of the tools that are used to give substance to the design.

    After sometime though, I got involved in the UX process defining the product too.

Gallery

Parallax backrounds effect/ FilmRoll Carousel / Materialize / Design

Gallery is the result of a design exercise focused on creating with the responsive framework materialize a demo site about art. The site has two functional tabs
(1)Current Exhibitions:There are 3 sections each one starts with a slider with the works of art that are included in the exhibition. (2)Artists: A small bio section for each one of the relevant Artists.

Details

For this site I used the Materialize: responsive framework taht is based on Google's Material Design. I also used the FilmRoll: Jquery Carosuel for the exhibition sliders. Added some css to have the images that are not active grayscaled. The images that I used were found online.

You can preview Gallery here.

The Raven

Parallax backrounds effect/ Design/ Poe’s poem.

This is the result of a design exercise focused on creating a site that “narrates” Edgar Alan Poe’s “The Raven”.

Details

By using scrolling parallax background effects the background-image changes as Poe’s poem “The Raven” unfolds. Reaching the to bottom, the background-image fades into one that shows the raven fly in the sky instead of sitting on the fence.
