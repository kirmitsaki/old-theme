Reviewing the Website in two steps

Step1: Expert Usability Reviews to find high level breaches
01. Visibility of system status MODERATE The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time.
02. Match between system and the real world POOR The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order.
03. User control and freedom MODERATE Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo.
04. Consistency and standards POOR Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions.
05. Error prevention MODERATE Even better than good error messages is a careful design which prevents a problem from occurring in the first place. Either eliminate error-prone conditions or check for them and present users with a confirmation option before they commit to the action.
06. Recognition rather than recall MODERATE Minimize the user's memory load by making objects actions, and options visible. The user should not have to remember information from one part of the dialogue to another. Instructions for use of the system should be visible or easily retrievable whenever appropriate.
07. Flexibility and efficiency of use MODERATE Accelerators -- unseen by the novice user -- may often speed up the interaction for the expert user such that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users. Allow users to tailor frequent actions.
08. Aesthetic and minimalist design POOR Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility.
09. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors MODERATE Error messages should be expressed in plain language (no codes), precisely indicate the problem, and constructively suggest a solution.
10. Help and documentation POOR Even though it is better if the system can be used without documentation, it may be necessary to provide help and documentation. Any such information should be easy to search, focused on the user's task, list concrete steps to be carried out, and not be too large.
Step2: Usability Testing to get a truer picture of the real problems people encounter

User observation gave valuable information on how users feel and act around the site.

Nο. of Users 10
Age 15-52
Occupation 4 developers, 2 art students, 1 high school student, 1 food designer, 1 graphic designer, 1 account manager
The task Users were asked to navigate through the site, comment on the design, process and how they feel while using the website and complete an order on Golden Cod website.

The following questions were asked so as to get their opinion on certain things:

  1. Would you recommend the website to a friend?
  2. How does the website compare to other similar sites?
  3. What do you like best about the website?
  4. What do you like least about the website?
  5. What do you find most frustrating about the website?
  6. If you could change one thing about the website what would it be and why?
  7. Overall, how easy to use do you find the website?
  8. How can the website be improved?
  9. If you were to review the website what score would you give it out of 10?

All the answers and other comments were gathered and added on screenshots of the website’s pages (see next images).

Usability testing results
  • Users depend largely on the home page to understand what the site is about, what they can do and how to do it.
  • Design and colours are irritating and not welcoming.
  • Not likely to make a payment in this website that fails to win their trust.
  • Home page doesn’t “sell” the food well
    • Not clear that there is a wide variety of food
    • Made the assumption: poor design == low quality food.
  • They found easy the process of making, editing and placing an order.
  • Show little to none interest for the rest of the pages.

A hypothesis based on test results

If the home page is appealing and manages to develop a relationship with the user, then the latter will be willing to place an order and return back in the future.

(In)Validating the Hypothesis

Create a design that is leaded by the hypothesis and then test with the produced prototype if the new design is more accepted by the users.

As the home page evolves, it must answer these questions:
  • What is this site about?
    • Clearly communicate the task that the users can achieve: "Order online from our wide variety of food".
  • How does it work?
    • As soon as the users are enamoured of the product they would like to know how to proceed.
  • Why are you better than the rest?
    Why should they trust you?
    • Sell your service in a short clever written paragraph. Aim to make the users feel comfortable and reassured about the quality of your services and make them fully trust you.

"Agia Anna" Bookstore Ongoing

Site Redesign

Going to add some images of the ux process soon.

Analysis of Previous Design

Checked Google Analytics and comments from the customers. The main problem that they report is the loading time and that they are overwhelmed of the quantity of information. Majority of users visit the website via mobile devices.

Points of improvement: Cleaner, responsive design that is guiding the users rather than bombing them with information. Should be easier to update content and maintain the site.

RE-Design

No e-shop site to sell products online but a website that will be the Bookstore’s online face.

Suggestion: Pageless Design.

(1) Storytelling.
Communicate with the users by telling your story in the single page website.

(2) Easy to inform.
The site has to hold the most important of the information due to space limitations.

(3) Kindles emotions.
With css tricks and smart interaction design the users will feel they entered the bookstore.

(4) Increases CRO.
Great, straightforward design, storytelling and clearly defined goals get more visitors from their ‘research’ stage to their ‘decision making’ stage.

(5) Easy to update and maintain.
Keeping the product catalogues in pdf files allows to update them anytime without having to make big changes in the code. Plus is faster to update the whole site since it’s only 1 page to deal with.

The prototype of a single-page site

The goal was the re-designed site to feel more like a story-telling rather than a boring product-displaying screen. Through the smart design of a single page only the most important information is displayed. The product catalogues often change so with keeping them apart from the code there will be less information to update and only one page to build/maintain.

Make the users feel like they entered into the bookstore.
